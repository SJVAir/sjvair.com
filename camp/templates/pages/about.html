About SJVAir

SJVAir is a network of low-cost air quality monitors providing real-time PM2.5 data to disadvantaged communities across the San Joaquin Valley. SJVAir is operated by a collaborative of non-profit organizations from Bakersfield to Stockton.

Air Monitor Testing & Calibration

Root Access provided the space for testing and calibration of SJVAir's Purple Air monitors before they were put into the field for data collection.

Two batches of PurpleAir monitors (60 total) were operated continuously on the roof at Root Access for 30 days each. During the first test period (12/10/2019 – 1/9/2020) the average hourly concentration was 36 ug/m3, with a range of 1-180 ug/m3. During the second test period (1/21/2020 – 2/20/2020), the average hourly concentration was 35 ug/m3, with a range of 1-85 ug/m3. The average correlation (R2) for all 60 monitors was 0.96. Only one monitor had an R2 < 0.90 (this monitor was returned to the manufacturer).

Particle count data from collocated PurpleAir monitors is regressed on regulatory monitor data to generate calibrations (correction equations) that can be applied to PurpleAir monitor data locally and regionally. In the example above, the regulatory monitor data is from CARB's Visalia station (Church St). A collocated PurpleAir at the same location was used to generate the "local" calibration. A collocated PurpleAir in Fresno (Garland Ave) was used to generate the "regional" calibration.
